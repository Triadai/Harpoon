This package contains support for the iPaq-based robot.

The MIT robot and tracked vehicle consist of:

  • Compaq Pocket PC (I use the iPaq 3600, with a 200 MHz StrongARM processor, 64 MB RAM, 32 MB Flash RAM.)
  • Compaq Pocket PC serial interface cable
  • Orinoco wireless card (IBM High Rate does not work.)
  • IBM 1 GB Microdrive w/PCMCIA sleeve
  • Cambridge Research Labs Camera Backpaq w/two PCMCIA slots, a battery, and a Philips camera
  • Jameco Servo Controller
  • Duratrax Evader ST w/ESC with steering assembly prebuilt (from Tower Hobbies)
  • Plantraco Desktop Rover
  • RadioShack 9-pin gender changer
  • RadioShack 9-pin null modem
  • 5 RadioShack hi-capacity (green) AA rechargable batteries
  • RadioShack 4 AA battery holder
  • RadioShack 1 AA battery holder
  • RadioShack 7.2V RC Car Battery Pack and Charger Combo
  • CVS Roll of velcro
  • CVS 9V battery
  • 2 CVS CR123A batteries

Wire the 5 AA batteries in series (red to black) and attach to the orange power cable of the Servo controller (red to +, black to -). Attach the gender changer and null modem to the Jameco servo controller. Plug the steering assembly servo cable into the socket closest to the null modem on the Jameco servo controller (with the white cable facing the power plug). Plug the ESC cable into the second closest slot. Attach the battery holders and servo controller to the body with velcro.

Slip the Compaq Research Labs sleeve on the iPaq. Insert the wireless card into the top PCMCIA slot. Insert the IBM Microdrive in its PCMCIA sleeve. Insert the sleeve into the bottom PCMCIA slot. Flash the software image onto the iPaq using the microdrive while the iPaq is charging. Plug the serial iPaq cable into the null modem on the car and the iPaq. Secure the iPaq on the car with velcro and unplug the power cable on the iPaq. Turn the camera on the sleeve to face the tracked vehicle.

Run the ground ATR software image on a laptop with wireless support.

The tracked vehicle is a Plantraco Desktop Rover and requires a 9V battery and 2 CR123A lithium batteries. Load the CR123A batteries into the rover and the 9V battery into the remote control. Turn on the remote using the black switch. Turn on the rover using the small black switch on the side. Plug the servo controller into the 5 AA batteries. Turn on the car using the small black switch on the ESC.

Congratulations, you're ready for robotic pursuit and evasion!
